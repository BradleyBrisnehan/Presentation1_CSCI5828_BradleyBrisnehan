Strengths

Rapid Application Development has several strengths:

  • Strong user input throughout all phases of development. Customer/User perception of the final design is well-established.
  • Issues or difficulties in system design can be detected much earlier. Missing functionality is also readily apparent with each iteration of prototypes.

These strengths make Rapid Application Development well suited for certain types of projects:

  • Small/Co-Locate Teams If the project team and customer group are small the amount and focus of feedback can be better controlled. A rule of thumb is that no design/review group is comprised of more than six people. In addition, these individuals should be co-located or frequent conference calls must be in place to facilitate quick decision making.
  • Vetted Architecture If the software architecture has been successfully employed by the team in the past. This past experience will make development of the backbone of the project easier and allow for more time meeting customer functionality requirements.
